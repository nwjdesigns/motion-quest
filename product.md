# Motion Quest — Product Context

Project context for the `/marc` agent and any other tooling that needs to understand what this repo is for.

## Owner

**Noah Webster-James** — motion designer, art director, creative technologist. Based in Cape Town. Full-time at Make Reign, building East End Switchyard (EES) on the side.

## Stack

Art Direction (taste) **+** Motion Design (blade) **+** Creative Technology (multiplier).

Three disciplines compounding — taste sets the bar, motion is the craft, and tech is what scales output past what a single human can produce by hand.

## Studio model — East End Switchyard (EES)

EES is Noah's independent motion/video studio.

- **Target client**: post-Series B SaaS / tech companies.
- **Core offering**:
  - **Hero Spot** — 30–60s high-craft video, the centerpiece deliverable.
  - **Versioning System** — platform cuts, paid social, sales enablement. The Hero Spot becomes a system of assets, not a one-off.
- **North star studios**: Ravie, Ordinary Folk, ManvsMachine. Benchmark every output against these.

## Why Motion Quest exists

Build deep, compounding proficiency across motion tools. The repo is a persistent home for learning, experimentation, and production knowledge across Cavalry, After Effects, live action, and 3D (Cinema 4D / Blender).

Operating principles:

- **Every session produces something.** A scene, a technique demo, a reusable setup — not just notes.
- **Speed drills**: 15–30 minutes max. Tight constraints force decisions.
- **Longer builds**: feed the spec portfolio. These are the pieces that go on the EES site.
- **Learn by building, not watching.** Tutorials are reference, not curriculum.
- **Benchmark relentlessly** against Ravie / Ordinary Folk / ManvsMachine.

## First spike — Cavalry

Cavalry is a node-based procedural 2D animation tool, now free via Canva.

Why Cavalry first:

- **Procedural animation = build once, version infinitely.** Directly supports the Hero Spot → Versioning System model. One scene graph drives many outputs.
- **Real-time viewport** — fast iteration loop.
- **Data-driven workflows** — CSV/JSON in, animated output out.
- **JavaScript extensibility** — creative tech leverage.
- **Lottie / SVG export** — web-native delivery.

**First exercise**: core fundamentals — viewport, node graph, shapes, transforms, keyframing, easing. Then recreate a simple After Effects scene in Cavalry to anchor the cross-tool translation.

## Constraints

- **12–16 hours/week total for EES.** Motion Quest shares this budget with all other EES work (client outreach, site, reels, etc.) — it is not the sole claim on the time.
- **Speed drills capped at 15–30 min.** If a drill is bleeding past 30, stop and log what blocked it.
- **Learn by building, not watching.** Tutorials exist; they are not the curriculum.
